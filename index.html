PROJECT 01 · FIN 4333 · DATASET CONSTRUCTION
Monthly Analytics-Ready Dataset Construction
Grameenphone Ltd. (DSE: GP) · Jan 2020 – Dec 2024

Built a clean, reproducible monthly financial dataset by combining three sources: daily stock prices (transformed to month-end closes & returns), annual firm fundamentals from official company reports (revenue, total assets, EPS), and Bangladesh macro indicators via the World Bank API (inflation, GDP growth). Applied a year-based alignment rule and ran 7-step quality checks covering missing values, duplicates, date consistency, data types, and suspicious values.

PROJECT 02 · FIN 4333 · GARCH VOLATILITY FORECASTING
GARCH Volatility Forecasting & Risk Memo
Target Corporation (NYSE: TGT) · 2020–2025

Estimated a GARCH(1,1) model on daily TGT log-returns to quantify market risk and produce a 5-day conditional volatility forecast for an investment committee. ADF confirmed stationarity (p < 0.01). Exhaustive AIC grid search across 72 ARIMA(p,d,q) permutations selected ARIMA(0,0,0) — pure random walk. Extreme non-normality (kurtosis 36.51, skewness −2.19) required a heavy-tailed Student's t distribution. Persistence α₁+β₁ = 0.8444 with a ~4.1-day shock half-life. Issued investment committee memo recommending delta-neutral hedging, long gamma/vega positioning, and 25% position de-leveraging.

PROJECT 03 · FIN 4333 · RETURN FORECASTING
ARIMA vs LSTM Return Forecasting
Target Corporation (NYSE: TGT) · 2020–2024

Forecast daily TGT log-returns using two competing models — classical ARIMA (AIC grid search + Ljung-Box diagnostics) vs. deep-learning LSTM (10-day sliding window, 50 units, validation loss monitoring). Strict chronological train/validation/test split (879/188/190 days) with no data leakage. Primary metric: test RMSE. ARIMA(1,0,3) recommended — matched LSTM on accuracy (RMSE delta <1.2%), outperformed on directional accuracy (54.7% vs 45.8%), and is simpler, faster, and easier to audit for a non-technical investment committee. Includes 30-trading-day return outlook (+0.41%) and full AI appendix.

Background

I am Anik Biswas, a Finance & FinTech major at United International University (UIU) in Dhaka, Bangladesh, currently serving as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant. My academic focus sits at the intersection of quantitative finance, financial analytics, and emerging FinTech.

My project work (FIN 4333 Financial Analytics, Team Hemisphere) spans the full quantitative pipeline — from raw data ingestion and cleaning, through statistical modelling, to actionable risk recommendations presented to investment committees. I have applied this pipeline to both DSE-listed equities (Grameenphone) and NYSE-listed stocks (Target Corporation), building familiarity with both frontier and developed market dynamics.

Every model I build is designed to be fully reproducible and auditable — fixed seeds, documented data sources, automated validation checks, and a detailed AI disclosure appendix. Instructor: Prof. Md Mohan Uddin, PhD.

Aspiring to grow as a Financial Analyst where I can combine analytical depth with real-world investment decision support.
